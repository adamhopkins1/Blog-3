Thoughts on the March 15 93rd Oscar Nominations, March 23rd 2021

Good Morning so this is a late post but Monday after my birthday was the Oscar Nominations Announcement that was on ABC's Good Morning America David Fincher's "Mank" was the most nominated film for this year with 10 nominations including "Best Picture" including nods for Gary Oldman as the title character Herman J Mankiewicz,Best Director (David Fincher) and Supporting Actress(Amanda Seyfried), But the surprises was for me was the 6 nominations for Florian Zellers's "The Father", Shaka King's "Judas and the Black Messiah" Lee Issac Chung's "Minari" , Chloe Zhao "Nomadland" Darius Marader "Sound of Metal" Aaron Sorkin's "The Trial of the Chicago 7" Emerald Fennell's "Promising Young Woman" which recieved 5 nods. Okay so this is interesting, I feel this years slate of films were outstanding and thoughoughly were quality experiences. In an unfortunate year where the impact of COVID-19 impacted the overall moviegoing experience each of the films mentioned managed to tell engaging, impactful, and fully- realized stories.It was dissapointing to not see films like "Nomadland" "Promising Young Woman" or "Trial Of The Chigago 7" in a theater such as my local "Cinemark" or "The Charles Theater" As I did in previous years. But overall I think this years class or nominees are top notch. I believe wholeheartedly this is Chloe Zhao's year as she has made a remarkable beautiful, and just excellent film "Nomadland" I never Thought a gifted and visionary director such as Chloe Zhao would be able to pull off a amazing trilogy of films such as "Songs My Brothers Taught Me", "The Rider" and now with "Nomadland" I have never been impacted heavily as this film has had on me. I feel this movie absolutely captures 2020 and the moment many movies either attempt or try to capture of dealing with the stages of grief and pain but the celebration of life in its purest like this movie has. I see this movie picking up awards in many categories mainly Best Picture, Best Adapted Screenplay, Best Cinemtography (Joshua James Richards camerawork is A-tier), Best Actress(Frances Mcdormand) and Best Director for Chloe Zhao whose three movies speak volumes of her vision and strong eye for telling stories.In other categories I see Best Original Screenplay going between Emerald Fenell or Lee Issac Chung for "Minari" As I know the AMPAS voting body is becoming more international. So it wouldn't be a surprise If they choose "Minari" as this movie could really capture the immigrant experience in a way that opposite of "Promising Young Woman", focus on domestic abuse and sexual assualt might have some semblance to the AMPAS branch but not towards the male body of the Screenwriting commitee.

Thoughts on the Producers Guild Association Predictions, March 24rd 2021

Tonight is the announcement of the winners for the 2021 Producers Guild Association, which is considered a precursor and predictor of the Academy Award for Best Picture. It will be tonight many of the predictions point to Fox Searchlight's Nomadland to be the top pick for the PGA which I do agree will be the PGA Winner but I wanted to posit a thought about tonights ceremony just to note there are ten nominees of the ten nominees there are 2 nomninees that are no nominated for best picture by the AMPAS now this will be a very precarious position for movies like "Mank", "One Night in Miami","Ma Raineys Black Bottom" and "Borat Subsequent MovieFilm", many of these films are distributed by streamers such as Amazon and Netflix many which falls outside the perview of the academy voters. Now as someone not intimate with the AMPAS secret voters or the inner workings of the preferential ballot commitees for the AMPAS voters. But I would suggest the academy voters are not taking kindly to the current conditions of viewing streamers digitally as opposed to the natural enviromnment of viewing films in a commnunal theater setting. I can see Nomadland winning the PGA which would be a 5th consecutive win for Fox Searchlight. However there are many chances for a "Green Book" scenario there is the possibility that "Trial of the Chicago 7" could be the PGA choice and would be the first win for Netflix as a streamer to win the PGA but also it could be Focus Features year where "Promising Young Woman" will be the chosen favorite but I do see "Nomadland" eventually winning PGA and DGA and Ultimately Oscar. But again there is a possibility "Trial of the Chicago 7" could upset or even "Minari" could have chances of gaining foothold towards April 25th 2021. I do also see that in leading up to the 25th of April there will be a final release a late entry in Florian Zeller's "The Father" from Sony Pictures Classics now headed up by Tom Rothman the former Founder of Fox Searchlight. There is a high chance the razor thin possibility that "Nomadland" and "The Father" could be neck in neck in terms of Academy Awards voters mindshare. It will be an interesting night for sure. The reason I added Sony Pictures "The Father" is due to the late (or long early addition) of Anthony Hopkins (Best Actor) Olivia Coleman(Best Supporting Actress) Correction: I want to add that Florian Zeller was not nominated for Best Director in his place is Thomas Vinterberg of the excellent film "Another Round" (Denmark), It has also been nominated for Best International Feature ("Another Round") it is peculiar to see this films recieve best director and this make me very curious about the choices in that category and would this prompt only Chloe Zhao to take Best Director and "The Father" to take Best Picture. I am really intrigued about this choice. But we will know more tonight at the PGA awards and On April 25th it should be an interesting ceremony on both fronts.-AH
